TipPrompt

This app is excellent. But it occurred to me that instead of the density, it should include either the theoretical pdf/pmf for the type of variable, best for seeing the differences between small and infinite samples.

Response

This is a fantastic pedagogical improvement. You are completely right: overlaying a theoretical density curve on top of a 15-sample histogram can actually be visually confusing.

To best illustrate the Law of Large Numbers (small sample vs. infinite sample), we should plot them side-by-side.

In this updated version:

  1. The left plot is strictly your empirical data (the small sample histogram) plotted as actual counts/frequencies.

  2. The right plot is the pure theoretical PDF (Continuous) or PMF (Discrete), representing the “infinite” sample.

  3. Crucially, I wrote a logic block to lock the X-axes of both plots to the exact same scale. This means if your 15-sample misses the “tails” of a normal distribution, the histogram’s axis will stretch to match the theoretical plot, making the visual comparison perfectly 1-to-1.
